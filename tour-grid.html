Our Austria holidays & tours

With their photogenic painted shutters and historic onion-domed churches, many of Austria’s alpine towns are almost too pretty to bear. And beyond the buildings, there’s a truly awe-inspiring landscape, where our holidays take you hiking into nature, snowshoeing into snowdrifts, and practising your yoga with fantastically fierce mountain views. Consider the waterside too. The Northern Lakes offer stillness and peace, and the River Danube weaves its way through the country, providing brilliant hiking and cycling routes along the way, through pretty villages and ancient byways. To enhance your Austrian alps experience, our holidays use local sources whenever they can – from accommodation to food, to employing the services of amazing guides, who know the mountains as well as they know their postcard-perfect towns.

Austria travel guide

Austria is most definitely about mountains and music. Just like a symphony, it has everything thrown into its musical mix: Alpine peaks, with Grossglockner the highest in Hohe Tauern National Park, boom out like a rousing brass section. The serene lakes of the north are the strings section, a series of soothing, tranquil movements that you can walk or cycle through in total awe of their purity and pitch perfect beauty.

Best time to visit Austria

If snow is your thing, late Nov-April is, on average, perfect for frosty fun. Austria likes its rain, so always bring an umbrella just in case. June-Aug bring beautiful summer weather, particularly the further south you go, but never too scorching. Of course the high mountains are always cooler, but with risks of lightning in summer. Christmas and New Year in Austria are magically festive, with glühwein and Christmas markets everywhere you go, no matter how remote. Hike in autumn to catch the wine harvest, with many festivals to accompany it.
